City Profile & Economic Performance

Geography & Heritage

Ahmedabad is India's first UNESCO World Heritage City. It covers 505 sq. km and serves as a vital logistics node due to its connectivity to the DMIC and proximity to Mundra Port.

Demographics: With a literacy rate of 88.3%, the city maintains a high-skill labor market supporting its ₹4.5 Lakh Cr+ district GDP (approx. 2023 estimates).

Economic Base

A global leader in Textiles and Pharma, Ahmedabad is diversifying into EV manufacturing and FinTech via the neighboring GIFT City corridor.

Performance: Consistently ranked in the top tier of the Ease of Living Index, AMC manages a revenue-surplus budget—a rarity among Indian ULBs.

Critical Urban Challenges

Water Security

Groundwater extraction at 152% exceeds sustainable levels. The dependence on the Narmada Canal necessitates massive investments in recycled water infrastructure.

Waste & Mobility

Bio-mining the Pirana landfill is a key environmental goal. While BRTS coverage is high, integrating "Last Mile" mobility through electric feeder services is the current policy focus.

Strategic Policy Intervention

Municipal Green Bond Issuance

AMC leveraged its AA+ rating to issue a ₹200 Cr Green Bond specifically for STP upgrades. This shift from state-dependency to market-based borrowing is a benchmark in Indian Municipal Finance.
